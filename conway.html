Conway's Game of Life

The Rules

Conway's game of life is a cellular automaton which "simulates" cellular underpopulation, overpopulation, reproduction, and society. That said, the rules for each generation of cells, according to Wikipedia, are the following:

  1. Living cells with less than two neighbors die.
  2. Living cells with more than three neighbors die.
  3. Living cells with 2 or 3 neighbors survive.
  4. Dead cells with exactly 3 live neighbors become living cells.

Structures within the Game

The rules for Conway's game give rise to lots of interesting structures. I've included here a few of my favorites that I learned about from the Cornell Math Explorer's Club. Use the clicking feature to construct some of them and watch them go!

Gliders

If you construct the shape to the right, or some reflection/rotation of it, then you'll create a glider. It will waddle diagonally across the screen. Keep an eye out for it in random games too. They often pop up.

F-Pentominos

This tetris block to the right looks simple but will actually evolve into a very robust colony! And it ends up spitting some gliders out in the process.

Boats

The boat is not particularly exciting. Indeed, it doesn't do anything. It's static. But that means it'll stay there until disturbed. It's like a lobster. Maybe it isn't the most interesting animal in the world but, barring sickness and predators, it will basically live forever!
